Concept Statement

The product's disguise is not a marketing decision — it is the design decision. Form, material, input method, and finish are all chosen to make the object unrecognizable in its context. The result is a personal wellness device that belongs on a bathroom shelf, not hidden in a drawer.

01
Design Rationale
Every decision in this concept is driven by a single principle: the object must be completely unrecognizable to an uninformed observer. This constraint generates the design.
Why no buttons?
Buttons are the primary visual signal that reveals a device's function. Their absence makes the object read as a passive object — a stone, a case, a compact. Full-surface capacitive touch replaces them without sacrificing control.
Why this form factor?
104 × 78 × 28mm — the exact footprint of a premium earbud case. The reference object is everyday and familiar. Nothing about the proportions signals the object's function, while the ergonomics remain deliberate and considered.
Why matte silicone?
Matte silicone is associated with premium consumer electronics and skincare packaging — not with this product category. It reads as "expensive object" before it reads as anything else. It is also body-safe, waterproof, and durable by nature.
02
Four Principles

The disguise
is the product.

Not a branding decision made after the product was designed. The form is developed around the constraint of non-recognition. The object passes inspection in a carry-on bag, on a bathroom shelf, in a hotel room. That invisibility is the primary feature — more important than any technical specification.

Principle 02
Touch is the only interface.
A button is a tell. The entire top surface is a single capacitive zone. Swipe upward to increase intensity. Swipe downward to decrease. Press firmly to power on or off. No modes. No memory settings. No pairing sequence. The interaction is self-evident within three seconds of first use — with no instruction.
Principle 03
Quiet at every intensity.
The motor specification is non-negotiable: brushless, under 35dB at maximum output. This is quieter than a normal conversation (60dB), quieter than a whisper at one metre (30dB). It is not a quiet-enough motor — it is a motor chosen specifically because it removes the sound as an element of the product experience entirely. Discretion is not a feature. It is a baseline requirement.
Principle 04
The shelf test.
The product must pass what we call the shelf test: set among skincare, candles, and ceramics on a bathroom shelf, it must belong. The colorways, finish, proportions, and embossed-only branding are all calibrated to this test. If it looks out of place next to a Aesop bottle or an Muji diffuser, the design has failed. This is the user environment. The product must earn its place in it.

07
Packaging
Outer Box

Unbranded kraft exterior.

Plain matte kraft card, 2mm thickness. No imagery, no product name visible on exterior. Shipping label only. A person handing a package to a doorman cannot identify contents from the exterior.
Inner Packaging

Formed recycled pulp tray.

Single-piece formed pulp insert holds the device and USB-C cable. No foam, no plastic. The product sits flush — removal requires tipping, which feels considered rather than grabbed.
Insert Card

One card. Three lines.

Single uncoated card, 120gsm. Front: wordmark. Back: three lines of instruction. Nothing else. The instruction is: Swipe up to increase. Swipe down to decrease. Press to on/off. All languages on one card.
08
Decisions & Rationale

What was
rejected
and why.

App connectivity: adds setup friction, reveals device category in app stores, and creates dependency on a phone charge state. Rejected.

Multiple intensity modes with memory: complexity for its own sake. The gesture interface gives continuous control — memory modes imply discrete levels, which is a downgrade. Rejected.

Branded exterior packaging: creates a moment of recognition at point of delivery that the product itself is designed to avoid. Rejected.

Decision
Why Shore A 40 specifically?
Shore A 40 is firm enough to maintain its form factor and not deform under grip pressure, while soft enough to feel skin-adjacent rather than plastic. It is the same durometer as high-end earphone ear tips and quality watch straps. It reads as premium material, not medical equipment.
Decision
Why not a glossy finish option?
Gloss silicone is associated with this product category. Matte is associated with consumer electronics, cosmetics, and ceramics. Every colourway is matte. There are no exceptions. Consistency of finish is part of the disguise system — one gloss option breaks the reading of the object.
